# ESE 6150 Final Race Submission Instructions

This repository is for your final race code. Please clean up your code, commit your final version, and write a README that explains how to run it and how your racing approach works.

Read these instructions before submitting.

---

## Why the README matters

The README is part of the submission. It should make your project easy to understand, build, and run.

A good README helps the course staff grade your work, helps future students learn from your project, and helps you remember what you built later. It can also be useful for people outside the class, such as other RoboRacer/F1TENTH teams, researchers, recruiters, or interviewers who want to quickly understand what you built and how much work went into it.

When you write it, assume it's for a student who understands the basics of ROS 2 and F1TENTH but has not seen your code before. Keep it clear and specific. Explain the decisions you made, the problems you ran into, what worked well, and what you would improve next.

---

## What to submit

Submit the following in your course GitHub repository:

1. Your final race code, cleaned up and committed.
2. A `README.md` at the root of the repository.

Late commits after the deadline will not be considered.

---

## What to include in the README

- List all team members and include each person's full name and GitHub username. You may also include LinkedIn, a personal website, or an email address if you want

- Include a short demo video or GIF. 10-60 seconds should be enough. The video can be hosted on YouTube, Google Drive, or another service with link sharing enabled

- Mention and describe your racing strategy

- Explain your approach to obstacle avoidance

- Mention the challenges you faced and how you addressed them

- Discuss your results and mention anything you would improve

- Explain how to build and run your code
